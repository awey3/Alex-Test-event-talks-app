1. Middle East: Ceasefire Collapse and Escalation in the Strait of Hormuz
The ceasefire between the United States and Iran has collapsed following a weekend of hostilities. After an Iranian strike on a container ship off the coast of Oman, both nations have asserted control over the Strait of Hormuz. In response, the U.S. Central Command (CENTCOM) conducted offensive strikes targeting dozens of Iranian military sites. President Donald Trump announced the reimposition of a U.S. blockade on Iran, declaring the U.S. as the "Guardian of the Hormuz Strait" and stating that fees would be charged to ships for safe passage. UN Secretary-General António Guterres warned of catastrophic consequences due to the escalation.

2. Death of U.S. Senator Lindsey Graham
U.S. Senator Lindsey Graham (R-SC) passed away on Saturday, July 11, at the age of 71, following a brief and sudden illness. Preliminary medical findings indicate the cause of death was an aortic dissection resulting from arteriosclerotic cardiovascular disease. President Trump ordered U.S. flags to be flown at half-staff in honor of the longtime senator.
